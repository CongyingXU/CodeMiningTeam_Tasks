Then the new configuration will be applied. During this, a lock on the logger is required. When now a new logger instance is requested by LoggerFactory, LoggerContext.getLogger(String) is called. The operation sets a lock on the root logger to create a new child logger. At the end the method LoggerContext.incSize() is called to increase the number of entries in the loggerCache. This method is declared as \"synchronized\" and needs a lock on LoggerContext.</p>\n\n<p>Requesting a new logger instance while starting a reconfiguration of logback leds to a deadlock.
